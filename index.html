About

I study Music Informatics—an interdisciplinary field at the intersection of music, psychology, and artificial intelligence. My research focuses on how the brain perceives, interprets, and generates music, drawing on computational methods and principles from cognitive psychology. I'm particularly interested in music cognition, the neuroscience of music, generative music systems, and real-time musical interaction.

Music has always been central to my life. Even as a student, I often found myself wondering why I was so deeply moved by music. That question led me to major in Psychology at Seoul National University, and eventually to Music Informatics. I'm now pursuing my lifelong goal: to transform my passion into a profession.
